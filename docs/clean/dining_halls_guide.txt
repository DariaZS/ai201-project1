Your Guide to the Best Dining Halls at Columbia

Eating on campus is a convenient way to consume a delicious variety of foods and meals from different cuisines. It is important to eat a balanced meal to succeed in your courses and to keep you energized throughout the day. Columbia Dining is here to support your nutritional needs while you are on campus.

The Student Dining Plan offers options for meals per week or term. The meals per term option allows you to spread your meal balance throughout the semester, which is useful if you don't eat enough on campus to use the weekly meals before they expire. If you do not want to commit to a dining plan, you still have the option to purchase a meal when you arrive at the dining hall.

Columbia Dining considers food and allergen safety a top priority. A registered dietitian on staff consults with Dining Plan holders to develop a personalized food allergy management plan. The culinary team is trained in proper food allergy handling, and menus are labeled so students can dine safely. In select dining halls, nuts and nut products are served exclusively at "nut zones" to limit cross-contact with other foods.

There are several themed dinners hosted by Columbia Dining throughout the year, such as French Night, Taste of the Caribbean, Campout, a Halloween-themed dinner, a Spongebob Event, Breakfast for Dinner, and Taste of Indigenous America. There is also the Battle of the Dining Halls, a friendly competition among the five Columbia Dining Halls: JJ's Place, Ferris Booth Commons, John Jay, Chef Mike's Sub Shop, and Faculty House. Each dining hall makes a signature dish presented to a panel of student and celebrity chef judges.

If you are experiencing food insecurity, contact The Food Pantry at Columbia for resources. All locations are accessible to any student, regardless of school affiliation, with no other criteria or financial requirements. The Lerner Hall location is on the 5th floor of Lerner Hall, open Tuesdays and Thursdays from 4:00 pm to 7:00 pm. The Medical Center location is on the 1st floor at 50 Haven Avenue, open Mondays and Thursdays from 6:00 pm to 9:00 pm.

At TC, there are two food-service locations on campus: Grace Dodge Dining Hall and Blue Java at Everett Cafe, but students can also access dining halls on the main Columbia campus.

Grace Dodge Dining Hall is one of three preserved original spaces on TC's campus, often compared to Hogwarts in architecture and design. One unique feature is a piano towards the back of the room, open for anyone to play. Events hosted there include the Annual TC Community Thanksgiving Dinner and the Holiday Pasta Dinner. For the 2024-2025 academic year, the two main stations are the made-to-order salad bar and the ramen bar. A meal is one swipe or one price and includes a customized salad or ramen bowl, plus fruit, dessert, and drink. The dining hall is no longer open on Fridays, operating only Monday to Thursday.

Everett Cafe is a great location to grab an espresso drink or tea between classes, or to get a salad, soup, or sandwich on the go. It is also a popular study spot, with the Music Department occasionally organizing jazz or blues live band performances.

Ferris Booth Commons is an excellent location for afternoon brunch, served until 2:00 pm. A BYO omelet from the omelet station is available during breakfast hours, which end at 11:00 am. The location has two floors of seating, with window seats overlooking the Great Lawn. During lunch, the pasta station and salad bar are very popular, along with a rotating action station offering burritos, noodles, falafel bowls, a pho bar, and more.

Dining locations on Teachers College campus include Blue Java at Everett Library Cafe, open Monday through Thursday 8:30 am to 7:30 pm and Friday 8:30 am to 2:00 pm, and Grace Dodge Dining Hall, open Monday through Thursday 11:00 am to 7:00 pm.

Dining locations on Columbia's main campus include Blue Java Cafe at Butler Library, Mudd Hall, and Uris Hall; Lenfest Cafe in Jerome Greene Hall; The Fac Shack near the John Jay gates; Ferris Booth Commons on the 3rd floor of Alfred Lerner Hall, open Monday through Friday 7:30 am to 8:00 pm, Saturday 9:00 am to 8:00 pm, and Sunday split hours; John Jay Dining Hall in John Jay Hall, open Sunday through Thursday 9:30 am to 9:00 pm; JJ's Place in the lower level of John Jay Hall, open daily; Chef Mike's Sub Shop in Uris Hall, open Monday through Friday 10:30 am to 10:00 pm; Chef Don's Pizza Pi in the Mudd Building, open Monday through Friday 8:00 am to 6:00 pm; and Faculty House on the second floor of Faculty House, open Monday through Wednesday 11:00 am to 2:30 pm.
