Register with Disability Services - Columbia Health

Columbia Health provides Disability Accommodations for students registered with Disability Services (DS). If you're interested in registering with DS due to a disability or temporary injury, begin by filling out the registration form or attending DS Drop-in Hours.

Students seeking academic or campus accommodations and support services from Disability Services are required to register with the office. If you received services in high school or at other universities, they do not automatically transfer to Columbia. You may register with DS throughout the year. However, if you're new to Columbia, you are encouraged to begin registration immediately, but no later than 3 weeks prior to the start of classes.

Who is eligible? Any Columbia student with a disability or temporary injury on the Morningside, Manhattanville, and Medical campuses is eligible to submit requests for academic or campus accommodations from Disability Services.

How do I register with Disability Services? To submit materials, you may send them via email, fax (212-854-3448), or drop them off in person at Wien Hall Suite 108A. As a brief overview, the steps consist of: completing and submitting a registration form; gathering and submitting disability documentation for all disabilities for which you're seeking accommodations; the Disability Services review process, which occurs for complete applications only and takes about 3 weeks; and meeting with a Disability Services Case Manager to review eligibility for accommodations and the policies and procedures for accessing them. Once fully registered, undergraduate students will be provided information about the online Accommodation Letter Request Form, while graduate students will connect with the Disability Services liaison from their school.

How much will it cost? There is no additional cost to receive accommodations from Disability Services. If required healthcare appointments or additional testing are needed to determine eligibility or documentation, those costs are determined based on the student's insurance plan.

What else do I need to know? Students who are unsure if they should register for Disability Services, or who would like specialized assistance with their application, can attend Virtual Drop-in Hours for a one-on-one, 15-minute discussion with a DS staff member. All accommodations are determined on a case-by-case basis. Submission of disability documentation does not guarantee eligibility for accommodations. Accommodations are not retroactive. DS is unable to implement accommodations that constitute a fundamental alteration to the requirements of a program or course.

Frequently Asked Questions

What information should I include in the registration form? The registration form is intended to help Disability Services better understand the request for accommodations, as well as the specific accommodations being sought. Students are encouraged to download the form so they can save progress as they go. There are no "right" answers when completing the registration form, particularly in the Student Narrative section. Students should focus on detailing their lived experience as a student with a disability as accurately as possible.

If I register with Disability Services, who is informed about my disability? Disability Services adheres to the standards set forth through the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA). Only confirmation of registration with DS and eligibility for accommodations is shared within the university on a need-to-know basis. To access any eligible accommodations in a course, the student's professors must be aware of the student's registration with DS and eligibility for accommodations, but the specific disability is not disclosed. There is no indicator of any kind on a transcript that a student has registered with DS.

What should I do if I'm not sure if my disability documentation meets the guidelines, or if I'm having difficulty securing disability documentation? Students seeking accommodations through DS are strongly encouraged to contact DS for additional guidance by email, by calling 212-854-2388, or by attending virtual drop-in hours.

I received accommodations previously, will I receive the same accommodations at Columbia? Students are encouraged to gather and submit historical documentation pertinent to their disability, such as an IEP or 504 Plan, prior evaluations, letters confirming registration and approved accommodations at a previous institution, or entrance exam accommodations. Historical documentation does not guarantee the same eligibility for accommodations at Columbia but is helpful nonetheless.
