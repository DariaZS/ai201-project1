Columbia Housing Strategy For Rising Sophomores

Housing can be stressful. Between navigating housing groups to understanding just how the housing lottery works, it can be overwhelming, especially as a rising sophomore. Columbia provides info sessions, but here is a condensed version for those who don't want to watch them.

One group equals one lottery number and one appointment time. Choose your group size based on your building preferences, such as whether you want a corridor double, a double within a suite, a 4-person apartment, or an 8-person apartment. Suite-style options include Carlton Arms and 600 W. 113th St (Nuss). You can find other residences on the housing website by filtering by typical residents, group size, and housing style.

The group leader will select rooms during the appointment, and everyone in a group must each sign their own occupancy agreement after selection to finalize the housing assignment. The group leader can be reassigned at any time, so choose someone who is available during the appointment time.

Room Selection typically runs from late March through late April, with sophomore selection happening in mid-April. If you don't get a room during Room Selection, you'll be put on the Guaranteed Assignment List and given a housing assignment by late July. Housing tries to keep groups together and takes building preferences into consideration, but nothing is guaranteed.

For more detailed information, check the Room Selection Information Session for Rising Sophomores and the Room Selection Group Strategy and Selection Info Session, both held annually by Columbia Housing.
